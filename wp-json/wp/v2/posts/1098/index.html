Presented by Mimburi Ninyangurra \u2014 BJ Murphy<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Mimburi means \u2018source\u2019 or \u2018continuous flow\u2019 and Ninyangurra means \u2018dreaming\u2019, so Mimburi Ninyangurra means \u2018Continuous Flow of Dreaming\u2019. <\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Jinibara Culture man BJ Murphy presents his first solo exhibition, with a series of sculptural installations and a songman performance in this not to be missed exhibition at Munnimbah-dja Welcome Place in Maleny. <\/p>\n

Through this work Bj Murphy will invite you into his ninyangurra, where  you will be immersed in the lived experiences of a Jinibara man returning to country. Mimburi Ninyangurra will sing out to and continue the sharing of culture in resistance to colonisation following in the footsteps of his ancestor Gaiarbau (Willie McKenzie) and many others whose footsteps have tread this path before him. <\/p>\n

The journey will start with the war cry handed down by Gaiarbau Bj will call out to country and wait for it\u2019s response and the continuous flow of cultural knowledge it offers when we listen.  <\/p>\n

After a solo songman performance you will enter the Munnimbah-dja gallery and witness more of the cherished gifts of knowledge handed down to the Jinibara people by Gaiarbau presented through a series of powerful canvas works.  <\/p>\n

Every piece in Mimburi Ninyangurra  is made with locally collected ochres, seeds and feathers, relating to and singing up Jinibara Country. <\/p>\n

Date: Sat  23 April \u2013 July 2 2022
\nTime: 9am \u2013 4pm, Thurs \u2013 Sat
\nLocation: Munnimbah-dja – Welcome Place 20 Coral st Maleny  (Jinibara Country) <\/p>\n
