Who are you and what do you create?<\/strong>
\nI\u2019m an artist living on the Sunshine Coast and I paint figurative and still life oil paintings.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

What materials and techniques do you use?
\nI predominately paint with oils on either canvas or wood. I use a direct approach of painting called \u2018Alla Prima\u2019 and enjoy the freedom in movement and expression the technique favours.<\/p>\n

What kind of inspirations go into your work?
\nI\u2019m inspired by many things \u2014 my natural surrounds, light, literature and people. I suppose my practice revolves a lot around searching for light. For me, light, like paint, sculpts objects. I\u2019m drawn to any imagery where light is transforming\/illuminating the space, whether it be dramatic or subtle.<\/p>\n

Lately I\u2019ve been painting a lot of still life assemblages, and I spend time cataloging imagery of living spaces and interesting light environments.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

In what space do you like to create most?
\nI work from my home studio in the Noosa Hinterland and I\u2019ve been lucky that wherever we\u2019ve lived, my husband makes sure there\u2019s a studio space. He built the home studio that I work in, and it has lots of natural light, which is perfect for me to create in. <\/p>\n

What has been your favourite or most important work to date?
\nMy favourite work that I\u2019ve painted recently is \u2018Amber Vase in the Morning Room\u2019. <\/p>\n
